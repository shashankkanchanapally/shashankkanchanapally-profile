Historic Places in India

Taj Mahal

Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh

Year Built: 1632-1653

Architect: Ustad Ahmad Lahauri

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the Yamuna river in Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Red Fort

Location: Delhi

Year Built: 1639-1648

Architect: Ustad Ahmad Lahauri

The Red Fort is a historic fort complex in the city of Delhi. It served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1857. The fort's massive red sandstone walls and intricate architecture are a testament to the grandeur of the Mughal era.

Hampi

Location: Karnataka

Year Built: 14th to 16th centuries

Architect: Various

Hampi is an ancient village in the state of Karnataka. It was once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Hampi is renowned for its well-preserved ruins, temples, and monolithic sculptures.
